Recommendation model comparison

Top-10 lists · time-based holdout

Four approaches were evaluated by training on transactions before Nov 1, 2011 and testing against actual Nov–Dec purchases. The hybrid model — collaborative filtering blended with content similarity — won on every metric.

Precision@10 and Hit Rate@10 by model

Full metric table

Precision@10 = share of the 10 recommended items the customer actually bought next.
Hit Rate@10 = share of customers with at least one correct recommendation.

Accuracy over time

Winning model · held-out months

Accuracy is highest in November, close to the training window, and softens in December — a normal pattern as customer behavior drifts further from what the model last learned.

Live output, hybrid model

One representative customer per segment, with their actual top-5 generated recommendations.

Methodology in one line: item-based collaborative filtering (co-purchase similarity) blended 70/30 with content-based filtering (TF-IDF on product descriptions), evaluated with a time-based train/test split to avoid leaking future purchases into training.

Data: UCI Online Retail transaction export, cleaned to 391,150 valid purchase line items across 4,334 customers and 37 countries. See the accompanying report and Python scripts for full detail.
